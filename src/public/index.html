Reynold's number is a dimensionless number that represents the ratio of the inertial force and the viscous force of a fluid. A fluid may be either a liquid or a gas. This number applies to any such fluid and is used to distinguish a fluid's laminar flow and its turbulent flow. Laminar flow is the smooth, steady, and predictable flow of a fluid---small disturbances quickly dampen. Turbulent flow is the fluctuating and chaotic flow of a fluid---predictions are difficult and disturbances do not quickly dampen.

The formula for Reynold's number is:

Re =
ρVD / μ

Where:

  • ρ is the density of the fluid
  • V is the velocity of the fluid
  • D is the diameter of the pipe the fluid is flowing through
  • μ is the dynamic viscosity of the fluid
